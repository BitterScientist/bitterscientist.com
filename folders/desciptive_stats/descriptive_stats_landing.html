Summarize your data with Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive Statistics: is a branch of statistics used to summarize features of a sample data set. This includes the central tendency and measures of variability. Descriptive statistics differs from inferential statistics in that it's focus is on describing the sample data, and not the larger population that is it believed to represent. Consequently, probability theory is not central in the mathematics of descriptive statistics.

Why is this important?
Descriptive statistics is a simple and powerful process at gaining insight into a given dataset, and is used in all industries that incorporate data in their operations. Descriptive statistics can be categorized as either univariate or multivariate:

Univariate analysis: describes the central tendency, dispersion, and shape of data distribution for a single variable.

  • Central Tendency:
    Example formulas: Mean, Median, Mode.
  • Estimates of Variability:
    Example formulas: Deviation, Range, Percentiles
    • Example plots: Histograms, Boxplots, Density Estimates, Frequency Tables.

Bivariate and multivariate analysis: are methods used to examine and describe the descriptive properties of the individual variables, AND the relationship between these variables.

  • Correlation:
    Example formulas: Pearson's correlation coefficient
    Example plots: Scatterplots, Line plots, Mosaic plots
