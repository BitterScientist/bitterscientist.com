Inferential Statistics

Inferential Statistics: is a branch of statistics that is used to make predictions, given a sampling of data. There are two main areas:

  1. Estimating parameters:
    Making inferences about a larger population based on a samples descriptive statistics.
  2. Hypothesis testing:
    The process of taking sample data and testing an assumption, and using the outcome to make statistical decisions.

Why is this important?
Inferential statistics is important because it enables statisticians to make predictions with a limited dataset. Unlike descriptive statistics, which provides a snapshot into the data analyzed, inferential statistics takes this information, and makes inferences about the larger population, or between several groups within that population.
