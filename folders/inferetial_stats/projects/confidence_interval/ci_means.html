Part 1: Confidence Interval for the Difference between two means

  • A confidence interval for the difference between two means specifies a range of values within which the difference between the means of the two populations may lie.

  • In many cases, analyst, scientist, or researchers are interested in gather data for two populations in order to compare them.

  • Confidence intervals and tests of significance are useful statistical tools to compare the difference between two population parameters.

  • A confidence interval for the difference between two means will specify a range of values where the difference between the mean of the population exists.

  • Examples:

    • Company that wants to estimate the differnece in the mean number of downloads of a specific app.

    • A researcher who wants to estimate the difference in efficacy between two Covid-19 vaccines in individuals that receivd them.

Example

  • The Super Bowl takes place Feb 7th, 2021, and we would like to know if there is a difference in the mean weights between the players participating in the game. (Tampa Bay versus Kansas City)

  • Question: Is there a significant differnce between the mean weight for the Tampa Bay and Kansas City Players?

  • Test the null hypothesis that the mu1 - mu1 = 0 versus the alternative that mu1 - mu2 is not zero.

  • Python numpy will be used in the calculation

Calculate the two-sample t statistic

  • Note the two sample t test is simplified so the numerator is only the mean of the sample populations as we are assuming that the mu1 and mu2 = 0
  • the k value is the degree of freedom for the smaller sample size minus 1
In [9]:
t_stat = (tampa_mean -  kc_mean )/ np.sqrt(np.square(tampa_std)/tampa_n + np.square(kc_std)/kc_n)
print(f'The t statistic is: {t_stat.round(3)}')

The t statistic is: 0.196

Find out what the p-value is from the t-Tables

  • Recall this is a two-sided test

  • we need to use the degrees of freedom (k) for the group with the smaller sample size and minus 1

The confidence interval for the difference in these two means is:(-13.329, 16.235)


Conclusion

  • The p-value for this 2-sided hypothesis is 0.8451 which suggest insufficient evidence that the mean weight of the football players is difference. (alpha = 0.05)
  • You can be 95% confident that the difference between your two population means (μ1 - μ2) lies between -13.329 and 16.235, with a critical value of 1.9917, standard error of 7.422.
