Background Info

Sinistrality
the state of having the left side (as the hand or eye) different from and usually more efficient than the right: left-handedness.

Do left handed individuals have shorter lifespans than right handed individuals in the US?
An article published in the New England Journal of Medicine (1) in 1991 suggest that left handed individuals (sinistral) have a decreased lifespan by about 9 years as compared to right handed individuals. Although this study was published 20 years ago, we will look into the data and determine the life span between right and left handed Americans.

The project goals The goal of the project is to test the claim made in article, that left handed individuals live shorter lives than right handed individuals, using bayesian statistics. Specifically, this post will calculate the average age at death between these two groups to determine if, and to what extent, is this age difference.

Test claim, do left handed people live longer than right-handed people.

  • There is an age gap between left-handed and right-handed people, in this study.
  • Right handed individuals lives on average 5.5 years longer than left handed individuals for the period in this dataset.
  • Reported rates of left-handedness were ~3-6% in the early 1900s and then increased to 10-13% in the late 1990s.
  • Right handed inviduals outnumber left handed individuals at a majority of ages, including older people in study.
  • Left-handed study was suggest that the US death data will have more right-handed deaths.
  • The numbers in this study are less than the study published, this may be due to the death data taking the entire US population into account, rather than just California.
  • Extrapolations used on younger and older age groups may not reflect the actual rates in those ages.
