Compare two investments using the Sharpe Ratio

First Posted: 1-28-2020
Last Updated: 1-28-2020

Background Info

Sharpe Ratio:
is a commonly used risk adjusted return ratio that compares the predicted returns between two investment opportunities, and the return per unit risk. In other words, how much risk is involved in a given return.
A Sharpe ratio >= 1: indicates that the returns on an investment is proportional to the risk taken
A Sharpe ratio less than 1: indicates that the returns on an investment is less than the risk taken.

Why is this important?

  • This is a simple formula that won a nobel price because of its utility.
  • It allows you to objectively compare across various investment opportunities.
  • Helps inform investors decide if the returns justifies the risk they may take.

    • The goal of this project
    is to highlight how the Sharpe ratio can be calculated in python, and how it can be used to compare two stock options. The Sharpe ratio is usually calculated for a portfolio, uses a risk free rate as a benchmark. A portfolio analysis project will be posted in the future to cover this topic more closely.
    Data:
    The Sharpe ratio will be calculated from 252 daily price records for Facebook and Amazon, while the S&P 500 will be used in lieu of the risk free rate, and will be used as the Information Ratio. Python and Jupyter Notebook was used for in this project.
