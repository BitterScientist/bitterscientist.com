Customer Segmentation using Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) Segmentation.

First Posted: 3-29-2020
Last Updated: 3-29-2020

Background Info

Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) Segmentation:
is the process of dividing customers into mutually exclusive groups based on three quantitative purchasing behaviors:
1. Recency: The last time a customer made a purchase.
2. Frequency: How often a customer makes a purchase.
3. Monetary: How much a customer spends.
This information is used by companies to gain getter insights into their customers, and in design engagement campaigns to increase sales, loyalty, and prevent customer churn.

Why is this important?

  • Allows a business to gain insights into customer groups.
  • Intelligence enables business to respond to customer needs.
  • Can help companies detect customer churn, so they may intervene beforehand.
  • Using python to segment customers is a powerful, scalable tool.

    • The goal of this project
    is to highlight how customers can be segmented into Recency, Frequency, and Monetary cohorts using python, and specifically:

    • Calculate Recency, Frequency, and Monetary values
    • Building Recency, Frequency, Monetary segments
    • Analyze RFM Segments
    Data:
    The data for this post was obtained from the UCI Machine Learning Repository. The Online Retail dataset is a transnational data set that contains the transactions occurring between 01/12/2010 and 09/12/2011 for a UK-based and registered non-store online retail store. The dataset contains 541,909 instances, and 8 attributes.
